Lines in 3-dimensional space

Summary

This section explains how to define and find equations for lines in space, which require both a point on the line and a direction vector. Unlike lines in the plane, which are typically described with a slope, lines in three-dimensional space require a vector to define their direction. This segment covers vector equations, parametric equations, and symmetric equations for lines, as well as methods to analyze relationships between multiple lines such as checking if they are parallel, intersecting, or skew.
