The Cross Product

Summary

This section discusses the cross product, a vector operation that results in a vector perpendicular to both input vectors. This operation is pivotal in determining orthogonality and has broad applications in geometry, physics, and engineering. We explore the definition, calculation methods, properties, and practical applications of the cross product.

Main Results
  • Cross Product Definition: For vectors 𝑢 and 𝑣 in 𝑅3, the cross product 𝑢×𝑣 is a vector perpendicular to both 𝑢 and 𝑣.
  • Calculation of Cross Product: Typically computed using the determinant of a matrix that includes the unit vectors 𝑖, 𝑗, and 𝑘, and the components of 𝑢 and 𝑣.
  • Properties of the Cross Product: Includes anticommutative and distributive properties, and its relation to the sine of the angle between the two vectors, indicating orthogonality when the cross product is zero.
  • Applications: Crucial for finding areas of parallelograms, volumes of parallelepipeds, and calculating torque in physics.
