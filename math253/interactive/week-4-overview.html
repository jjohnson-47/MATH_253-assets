Section 12.4: Unit Tangent and Normal Vectors

Separating direction from speed in vector-valued motion

Aurora Metaphor

Think of unit tangent vectors as compass needles embedded in the flowing aurora, always pointing in the direction of motion with exactly unit length. The unit normal vectors point perpendicular to this flow, showing which way the aurora is turning as it curves through the sky.

Unit Tangent Vector Definition
$$\mathbf{T}(t) = \frac{\mathbf{r}'(t)}{||\mathbf{r}'(t)||}$$
Key Insight: The unit tangent vector $\mathbf{T}(t)$ gives us pure direction by normalizing the velocity vector $\mathbf{r}'(t)$. It always has magnitude 1, regardless of how fast we're moving.
Principal Unit Normal Vector
$$\mathbf{N}(t) = \frac{\mathbf{T}'(t)}{||\mathbf{T}'(t)||}$$
Geometric Meaning: $\mathbf{N}(t)$ points in the direction that $\mathbf{T}(t)$ is changing. It's always perpendicular to $\mathbf{T}(t)$ and points toward the "inside" of the curve.

Key Concepts You'll Master:

  • Computing unit tangent vectors by normalizing velocity
  • Finding unit normal vectors using the derivative of $\mathbf{T}(t)$
  • Understanding why $\mathbf{T}(t) \cdot \mathbf{N}(t) = 0$ (orthogonality)
  • Decomposing acceleration into tangential and normal components
  • Interpreting $a_T$ (change in speed) vs $a_N$ (change in direction)

Section 12.5: Arc Length and Curvature

Measuring distance traveled and rate of turning

Aurora Metaphor

Arc length measures the total distance an aurora stream travels as it flows across the sky. Curvature tells us how sharply the aurora bends‚Äîthink of a gently curving arc versus a tight spiral. High curvature means the aurora is changing direction rapidly; low curvature means it's flowing in a relatively straight path.

Arc Length Function
$$s(t) = \int_a^t ||\mathbf{r}'(u)|| \, du$$
Physical Meaning: $s(t)$ represents the total distance traveled along the curve from time $a$ to time $t$. It's like reading the odometer in your car.
Curvature Definition
$$\kappa = \frac{||\mathbf{T}'(t)||}{||\mathbf{r}'(t)||} = \frac{||\mathbf{r}'(t) \times \mathbf{r}''(t)||}{||\mathbf{r}'(t)||^3}$$
Geometric Interpretation: Curvature $\kappa$ measures how fast the unit tangent vector is changing. It's the "bendiness" of the curve‚Äîlarge $\kappa$ means sharp turns, small $\kappa$ means gradual curves.

Advanced Concepts:

  • Arc length parameterization: when $||\mathbf{r}'(s)|| = 1$
  • Alternative curvature formulas for different curve types
  • Radius of curvature: $\rho = \frac{1}{\kappa}$
  • Osculating circle: the "best fit" circle at each point
  • Relationship between curvature and normal acceleration: $a_N = \kappa v^2$
